Full Recipe: One-Pan Lemon Garlic Chicken with Broccoli, Red Peppers, and Brown Rice
- Serves: 4 | Time: ~35-40 minutes
- Ingredients used
  - Chicken thighs: 4-6, skin-on or skinless, depending on preference
  - Brown rice: 1 cup (uncooked)
  - Broccoli florets: 2 cups
  - Red bell pepper: 1 large, sliced
  - Lemon: 1 large (zest + juice)
  - Garlic: 2-3 cloves, minced
  - Olive oil: 1-2 Tbsp
  - Chicken broth: 2 1/2 cups (or water)
  - Salt & pepper
  - Optional: 1/2 tsp paprika or dried oregano for extra warmth
- Steps
  1) Preheat a large skillet with high sides over medium-high heat. Season chicken thighs with salt and pepper.
  2) Add 1 Tbsp olive oil. Sear chicken 3-4 minutes per side until browned. Remove and set aside.
  3) In the same pan, add the remaining oil, minced garlic, sliced red pepper, and broccoli. Sauté 2-3 minutes until the veg start to brighten and soften.
  4) Stir in the brown rice, then pour in chicken broth. Bring to a simmer.
  5) Return the chicken to the pan, nestling it into the rice. Cover and simmer about 25-30 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the chicken reaches 165°F (74°C).
  6) Stir in lemon juice and zest. Adjust salt and pepper to taste. If you like, finish with an extra squeeze of lemon.
- Nutrition snapshot (rough estimate per serving): 420-460 kcal, 25-30 g protein, 50-60 g carbs, 12-14 g fat (depends on chicken skin-on vs skinless and exact rice amount).

Full Recipe: Lemony Chicken Thighs and Rice Skillet with Broccoli & Peppers
- Serves: 4 | Time: ~30-40 minutes
- Ingredients used
  - Chicken thighs: 4
  - Brown rice: 1 cup (or use par-cooked rice if you prefer)
  - Broccoli: 2 cups florets
  - Red pepper: 1, sliced
  - Lemon: 1 (juice + zest)
  - Garlic: 2 cloves, minced
  - Olive oil: 1-2 Tbsp
  - Chicken broth or water: 2 cups
  - Salt & pepper
- Steps
  1) Season chicken with salt and pepper. Brown in a skillet with olive oil, 3-4 minutes per side. Remove.
  2) In the same skillet, add garlic, peppers, and broccoli; sauté 2-3 minutes.
  3) Add rice and broth; bring to a simmer. Return chicken on top, cover, and cook 15-20 minutes until rice is tender and chicken is cooked through.
  4) Finish with lemon juice and zest, plus a pinch more salt if needed.
- Nutrition snapshot (rough estimate per serving): 400-450 kcal, 25-30 g protein, 50-55 g carbs, 12-14 g fat.

Full Recipe: Chicken and Rice with Broccoli (One-Pot)
- Serves: 4 | Time: ~35-40 minutes
- Ingredients used
  - Chicken thighs: 4
  - Brown rice: 1 cup (dry)
  - Broccoli florets: 2 cups
  - Lemon: 1 (juice + zest)
  - Onion or garlic as you like (optional)
  - Chicken broth: 2 1/2 cups
  - Olive oil or butter
  - Salt & pepper
- Steps

  3) Return chicken, simmer covered 25-30 minutes until rice is tender and chicken is cooked.
  4) Finish with lemon juice and zest; season to taste.

1) Creamy Lemon-Garlic Pan Sauce
- Flavor: bright lemon with velvety garlic notes.
- What you need (approx): butter or olive oil, 2 cloves garlic, 1 lemon (juice + zest), 1/2–1 cup chicken broth, splash of cream or yogurt (optional).
- How to use: after you sear the chicken, remove it. In the same pan, melt butter, add minced garlic 30 seconds, pour in broth and lemon juice, simmer 2–3 minutes. Stir in a little cream or yogurt off heat if you like it extra creamy. Return chicken and veggies to coat.

2) Honey-Garlic Soy Glaze
- Flavor: savory-sweet glaze with a zing from garlic and lemon zest.
- What you need (approx): 2 Tbsp soy sauce, 1–2 Tbsp honey (or brown sugar), 1–2 cloves garlic, optional ginger, a pinch of lemon zest.
- How to use: whisk the glaze and simmer a couple minutes until glossy. Brush over the chicken and toss the broccoli/peppers in during the last 3–5 minutes to glaze. If you want it thicker, whisk in a 1 tsp cornstarch slurry.

3) Tahini-Lemon Drizzle (dairy-free option)
- Flavor: nutty, tangy, creamy without dairy.
- What you need (approx): 2–3 Tbsp tahini, 2–3 Tbsp lemon juice, water to thin, 1 small garlic clove, pinch of salt, chili flakes (optional).
- How to use: whisk until smooth with enough water to reach a pourable sauce. Drizzle over plated dish or toss with the chicken and veggies right before serving.

4) Mustard-Yogurt Dill Sauce (bright and tangy)
- Flavor: tangy, creamy, herby.
- What you need (approx): 1/2 cup Greek yogurt (or dairy-free yogurt), 1–2 Tbsp lemon juice, 1–2 tsp Dijon mustard, fresh dill or parsley, salt and pepper.
- How to use: whisk together and serve on the side or spoon a dollop over the chicken and veg. If dairy-free, swap in dairy-free yogurt.

Quick tips to add the sauce to the first recipe
- Do the sauce in the final 5–7 minutes of cooking so flavors stay bright and the chicken stays tender.
- If you’re using a glaze (Option 2), you can brush it on during the last few minutes and let it reduce a bit for a sticky coating.
- Taste and adjust: more lemon for brightness, more salt if needed, or a touch of honey if you want more sweetness.
