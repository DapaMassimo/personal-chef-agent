Full Recipe: Lemon Pepper Chicken with Broccoli, Red Peppers, and Brown Rice (One-Pan Inspired)
- Serves: 4
- Time: 30-40 minutes

- Ingredients used
  - Chicken thighs (boneless, skin-on or skinless) – about 4-6 pieces
  - Broccoli florets – 2 cups
  - Red bell pepper – 1 medium, sliced
  - Lemon – zest and juice (from 1 lemon)
  - Brown rice – 1 1/2 cups dry (yields ~3 cups cooked)
  - Olive oil – 2–3 tablespoons
  - Garlic – 2 cloves, minced
  - Chicken broth or water – 2 cups (for rice)
  - Salt and pepper – to taste
  - Optional: paprika or chili flakes, dried oregano or Italian seasoning

- Steps
  1) Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).  
     Pat chicken thighs dry, then season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of paprika or Italian seasoning.
  2) In a large sheet pan, spread broccoli florets and sliced red pepper. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with a little salt, and toss to coat.
  3) Nestle the seasoned chicken thighs among the veggies. Squeeze over lemon juice and scatter lemon zest on top.
  4) Roast in the oven for 20–25 minutes, or until the chicken reaches 165°F (74°C) and the vegetables are tender.
  5) Meanwhile, cook brown rice: in a pot, combine rice with chicken broth (or water) and a pinch of salt. Bring to a boil, reduce to a simmer, cover, and cook about 35–40 minutes until tender and liquid is absorbed.
  6) When chicken and veggies are done, taste and adjust salt/pepper. Serve the roasted chicken and vegetables over the brown rice, with an extra squeeze of lemon if desired.

- Nutrition snapshot (approx.)
  - Per serving: 420-520 calories; protein ~34 g; carbohydrates ~45–55 g (mostly from brown rice and peppers); fat ~10–14 g; fiber ~5 g
  - Notes: exact numbers depend on chicken thigh fat level and rice variety.

- Source: Inspired by One-Pan Lemon Pepper Yogurt Chicken and Rice (adapted for brown rice and extra peppers) [web:3]

Tips
- If you’d rather marinate: mix lemon juice, zest, olive oil, garlic, salt, pepper, and a pinch of paprika and toss the chicken in the marinade for 15–30 minutes before roasting.
- Red peppers add color and sweetness; you can swap in other quick-cooking veggies if you have them (zucchini, snap peas, or mushrooms).
- For extra sauce: whisk a little plain yogurt or a splash of chicken broth into the roasting pan with 5 minutes left; spoon over the chicken and rice.
- Storage/swaps: refrigerate leftovers in airtight containers for up to 3 days. Reheat gently in the microwave or a skillet; the rice may dry a bit—add a splash of broth or water to loosen.

1) One-Pan Lemon Pepper Yogurt Chicken and Rice with Lemon Yogurt Sauce
- Serves: 4 | Time: 30-40 minutes
- Base ingredients (as in the original one-pan): chicken thighs, broccoli, red pepper, lemon, brown rice, olive oil, garlic, salt, pepper
- Sauce ingredients
  - 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  - 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  - 1/2 teaspoon lemon zest
  - 1/4 teaspoon salt, optional
  - Pinch of black pepper or a small pinch garlic powder (optional)
- Steps
  1) Prepare base dish as in the original recipe (roast chicken with broccoli and peppers; cook brown rice separately).
  2) In a small bowl, whisk together yogurt, lemon juice, lemon zest, salt, and pepper.
  3) After roasting, remove pan from oven. Spoon or drizzle the lemon yogurt sauce over the chicken and veggies, or serve on the side with the rice.
  4) Stir any leftover sauce into the warm rice if you like extra tang.
- Nutrition snapshot (approx): ~520-620 calories per serving (base 420-520 plus sauce ~80-100 extra); protein ~34 g; carbs ~45-55 g; fat ~12-18 g
- Source: Lemon yogurt sauce ideas and applications [web:1]

2) One-Pan Lemon Pepper Yogurt Chicken and Rice with Garlic Lemon Butter Sauce
- Serves: 4 | Time: 30-40 minutes
- Sauce ingredients
  - 2 tablespoons butter
  - 2 cloves garlic, minced
  - 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  - 1/4 cup chicken broth (or water)
  - Salt to taste
- Steps
  1) Make the base dish as described.
  2) In a small skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté 30-60 seconds until fragrant.
  3) Add lemon juice and chicken broth; simmer 1–2 minutes to reduce slightly.
  4) Drizzle the garlic-lemon butter sauce over the roasted chicken and veggies just before serving, or pour it into the pan to coat the ingredients lightly.
  5) Serve with the brown rice; drizzle a little extra sauce on top if you like.
- Nutrition snapshot (approx): ~570-650 calories per serving (adds a bit more fat from butter); protein ~34 g; carbs ~45-55 g; fat ~22-28 g
- Source: Garlic lemon butter sauce ideas and general chicken-pan sauces [web:2]

3) One-Pan Lemon Pepper Yogurt Chicken and Rice with Lemon Tahini Sauce
- Serves: 4 | Time: 30-40 minutes
- Sauce ingredients
  - 2 tablespoons tahini
  - 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  - 1 teaspoon olive oil
  - 2–3 tablespoons warm water (to thin)
  - Pinch of salt
  - Optional: 1 small garlic clove, minced
- Steps
  1) Prepare the base dish as before.
  2) In a small bowl, whisk together tahini, lemon juice, olive oil, warm water, salt, and garlic if using. The sauce should be pourable but not too thin; adjust with water or tahini as needed.
  3) Drizzle or spoon the tahini-lemon sauce over the roasted chicken and broccoli peppers. Toss the rice with any pan juices for extra flavor.
- Nutrition snapshot (approx): ~520-640 calories per serving (depends on tahini content); protein ~34 g; carbs ~45-55 g; fat ~16-22 g
- Source: Lemon tahini sauce ideas and examples [web:3]

- Storage: refrigerate any sauce in a sealed container up to 4–5 days. Reheat gently, adding a splash of water or broth if it thickens.
